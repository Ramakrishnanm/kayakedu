Bottom line is simply: Italy is awesome! Business

Italy is home to the oldest university in the world, University of Bologna; and, since its inception, the educational system never stopped growing and improving. With student discounts, thousands of Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees, and a beautiful and diverse country, there’s no wonder students pick Italy as their international destination.
It might seem there aren’t enough words in the world to express what a beautiful and breath- taking experience  studying in Italy  can be; but, since someone felt the need to describe this feeling of shortness of breath as an illness (No, really… Google “Florence syndrome”), you can only assume what a marvellous period in your life this will be.

Why study in Italy?

Praising Italy might be the most complicated thing in the world: where do you even start?. Do you mention a bunch of famous Italians, like da Vinci, Borgia, Versace, and Vivaldi? Or just mention the fields they revolutionised, like Architecture, Music, Engineering, and Fashion? I could just focus on the brilliant landscapes and the UNESCO World Heritage sites, or how awesome Italian people are, with their pastas, wines, and signature hand gesture, but it would be redundant.

Accommodation in Italy

Out of the total monthly expenses of EU students, they usually pay around 35 % on accommodation, 9% on transportation and around 12% on tuition fees. Rates for accommodation in Italy are in the international range of 200 – 300 EUR/month.

Documents Required

For student Visa:

  • Original passport &amp; Visa photographs

  • Original and attested photocopy of the acceptance letter by the Italian School or Institution, indicating the duration of the course.

  • Dichiarazione di Valore (Statement of value)

  • Original education certificates - Proof of support means in Italy of no less than 5750 euros for one year or 442.30 euros for each month of the academic year in cash or travelers checks or international credit/debit card. 

  • Card with limits and bank statements for the last six months.

  • Proper proof of boarding and evidence of accommodation .

  • Travel insurance abroad.

  • Original scholarship test: When the amount of the scholarship does not meet the minimum requirement of (5750 euros for one year or 442.30 euros for each month of the academic year). 

  • The difference must be presented in cash or travelers checks or credit card with limits and bank statement.
